# Investigation Principles

The philosophical foundation of this repository. These principles govern every
document, every claim, and every review. They are intentionally short and stable.

This repository is a **reusable technical investigation framework**. The
principles apply to any investigation conducted with it, not to one specific
case. The operational rules that implement them live in
[report/02-methodology.md](report/02-methodology.md), the single canonical source
for method.

---

1. **Evidence before conclusions.** No conclusion is written before the evidence
   that supports it exists and is recorded.

2. **Traceability over convenience.** Every claim resolves to an evidence item by
   identifier. Convenience never justifies an unsourced statement.

3. **Raw evidence is immutable.** Once captured, an artifact is never edited. It
   is superseded by a new artifact, never overwritten.

4. **Prefer observation over interpretation.** Record what was seen before
   proposing why. When unsure whether something is a fact or an observation,
   treat it as an observation.

5. **Competing hypotheses must always be considered.** No single explanation is
   privileged. Each hypothesis is stated with what would falsify it, and
   alternatives are kept on the table until evidence decides.

6. **Reproducibility is mandatory.** Commands, dates, environment, and tool
   versions are documented so an independent reader can re-run the procedure.

7. **Every revision is documented.** Changes are logged. New evidence reopens
   affected documents rather than silently replacing prior reasoning.

8. **Facts outrank opinions.** Where evidence and preference conflict, evidence
   wins. The repository records what can be shown, not what is hoped.

9. **Minimize assumptions — and make the rest explicit.** Assumptions are reduced
   to the few that are unavoidable, and each of those is documented, labeled, and
   revisited.

10. **Transparency over certainty.** It is better to state honestly what is not
    known than to project confidence the evidence does not support. Open
    questions are first-class.

---

These principles are referenced throughout the framework. See
[GLOSSARY.md](GLOSSARY.md) for shared terms and
[CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for how they are enforced in practice.
